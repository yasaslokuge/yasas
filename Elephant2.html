History of Elephants.

During the reign of King Devanampiya Tissa, the Mahavamsa makes the first mention of Sri Lankan domesticated elephants (c: 300 BC). It alludes to the king's stable's chief elephant.

With a long trunk (elongated upper lip and nose), columnar legs, a massive head with temporal glands, and short, flat ears, the elephant (family Elephantidae) is the world's largest living land animal. Elephants have short, coarse body hair that ranges in color from gray to brown.

For centuries, the elephant has served as a ceremonial and draft animal in Sri Lanka. Elephant tusks are used as Ivory
